Lana Condor in Bathing Suit Goes &quot;Anywhere With You&quot; Celebwell

 Posted on April 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  435 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Lana Condor is an actress best known for her lead role in the To All The Boys I've Loved Before film series. And based off of her most recent Instagram post, she's got a boy to love her now. Condor enjoyed a vacation with her boyfriend, Anthony De La Torre, and shared a series of photos of their time at the beach. (One of these photos is of her posing in the water in a bathing suit.) The caption: "Anywhere with you." Condor shared this series of photos of her on vacation, where one of her activities was hitting the golf course. Condor is seen swinging her club, and driving in a golf cart, in two photos. She captioned the post, "I keep trying to think of a funny caption, but I just keep puttering around."

Condor revealed in this Instagram post that she struggles with acne for most of her life. In order to help her with this, she has to take care of her skin. Condor advertised Netrogena's Oil Free Acne Wash product. Condor says that it is, "THE product that has been part of my clear skin arsenal and daily routine for years!"

Condor revealed to SELF that at the beginning of the pandemic, she didn't workout as much. However, as time went on, she says that she changed her mindset on fitness. "I was like, no, I want to take back my control in the only way that I know I can, which is in my health," she says. "Because there's so much uncertainty outside. I want to be able to feel a little bit of stability."

Condor revealed to SELF that she struggles with body dysmorphia, which causes her to develop anxiety and obsession over her figure. However, she says that she's working hard to value herself and not get insecure about her body. "I have to really, really, really tell myself that I am beautiful and my talent is valid. And I might look a little bit different than I did, whatever, a year ago." 

Condor revealed to Shape that she is a big fan of Zumba, and that it helped get her out of a perfectionist mindset she had from doing ballet. "Zumba was really a place where I could just have an hour to let go and move my body for the fun of it and every movement doesn't have to be 'spot on.'"
